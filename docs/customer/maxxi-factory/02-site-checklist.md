This is a short list of things we need from your site to connect your FrameCad
machine. Most of it takes five minutes — just a few files and photos.

---

## Must have — we cannot start without these

- [ ] **Sample job files (2+)** — copy the files your operator loads onto the USB
  stick today. We need at least two different jobs (e.g. one wall frame and one
  truss). **Especially need the `.rfy` files** — this is what the FrameCad
  machine actually reads. Also any `.csv`, `.nexa`, or `.xml` files you have.
  _Email or LINE to Gerard._

- [ ] **USB folder path** — when the operator plugs in the USB stick, what folder
  does the FrameCad machine look for job files? Is it the root of the drive, or
  a specific folder? _Ask the operator — one sentence answer._

- [ ] **Machine model number** — the model is printed on a plate on the machine
  (e.g. F325iT, F450iT, ST950H). _A photo of the plate is fine._

---

## Nice to have — helps us move faster

- [ ] **FrameCad software version** — what version of Steelwise or Structure is
  installed on the design computer? _Screenshot of the About/Help screen._

- [ ] **Current Nexa subscription** — are you on Nexa Base (free), Builder ($1,000/yr),
  Podium ($2,000/yr), or Enterprise ($3,000/yr)? _This tells us exactly what
  software cost we can eliminate for you._

- [ ] **Design workflow** — how does a job get from design to the machine today?
  e.g. SketchUp → Steelwise → USB stick? Or something different?
  _One or two sentences._

- [ ] **Photo of the machine's USB port area** — we need to see where the USB
  stick plugs in, and how far it is from the nearest power point. This tells us
  what cable length we need for the Pi Zero.

- [ ] **Factory WiFi** — is there WiFi coverage near the machine? What network
  name? _The Pi needs WiFi to receive jobs._
